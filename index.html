Policies + Shop Etiquette
Materials List Safety Sheet

This course focuses on learning the basics of monotype printing, silkscreen, intaglio, and chine collé. We will work on project-based assignments learning and practicing in the service of idea generation. With each printmaking technique we will introduce a new conceptual theme that relates back to the approach. These themes may be present in the work of both historical and contemporary artists that utilize printmaking in their practice. In class discussion will be central, and we will consider art history and multiple approaches to the process of printmaking. The instructor and teaching assistants will demonstrate various techniques throughout the course. Prior printmaking experience is not necessary.

Week 1
Wednesday, August 24th
Theme — Singularity

Introduction to the course, training day to gain access to the studios, review syllabus and look at examples of printmaking that we will be dealing with this semester in this course.

    Discussion on Singularity:
  • What constitutes a print?
  • How could you interpret the theme of Singularity in printmaking?
  • What is the context for a single print vs. a multiple?

Artists considered: Charline von Heyl’s abstract prints, Cullen Washington’s Collographs, Tara Donovan’s Slinky relief prints Bea Parson's inky monotypes Karen Lederer's colorful monoprints

Assignment 1 Prep:
  1. Before next class watch MoMA Video on Edgar Degas’ monotype
  2. Write a description in your sketchbook on the basic differences of monoprint vs monotype
  3. Look for examples of monoprints and monotypes and see what you can find that interests you, print out and put in your sketchbook

Monotype Demo Day:
Watercolor mono printing with plexiglass and Dura-Lar sheets, applying gum arabic, soaking paper, utilizing various techniques and materials and a brief discussion on the differences between Monotype and Monoprint

Assignment 1: Monotype Printing
Outside of class time, by applying the theme of Singularity print a suite of (at least) 3 monotypes and 2 ghost prints. This print should include the white of the paper, at least (if not more) two other colors and black. You should make your prints by experimenting with the techniques demonstrated in class before next week.

Wednesday August 31st
Theme — Singularity

Critique day
Complete Assignment 1 Monotype Print be ready to talk about your work, review Four Steps in Art Criticism

Artists Statement 1:
Hand in lists for Statement and you should have read Nyland Blake text
Sketchbook due in class for review, it should include research, notes, process
Week 3
Wednesday September 7th
Theme — Evolution
    Discussion on Evolution:
  • What makes an Edition Variable?
  • How could prints evolve through a body of work?
  • How can destroying the image create new possibilities and variations?

Artists Considered: Louise Bourgeois’ print process, Rochelle Feinstein’s print box, Rirkrit Tiravanija’s scroll project.

Assignment 2 Prep:
  1. Watch this video from MoMA on Louise Bourgeois’ print processes
  2. Take a look at Wingate Studio to research what is possible with Intaglio
  3. Start to research and consider imagery that you’d like to work on for the assignment

Intaglio Demo day:
how to file the plate, how to paint on the ground, preparing paper, inking and wiping then printing

Assignment 2: Intaglio Printing
Applying the theme of Evolution create two copper plates 8 x 10 size with images that you will create a variable edition —Make your designs on both plates that can be registered together, First print at least one proof of each plate, save one print, on the second print draw, paint or collage to create a new image (2 prints) —Secondly using this new image as a starting point etch on top of your used plates, and then print at least one proof of this new image (2 prints) At the end of this process you should have 4 prints *extra credit if you print an extra proof of your new image and draw, paint or collage on top of your new image for a final total of 6 prints

Week 6
Wednesday September 28th
Theme — Multiplicity
Discussion on Multiplicity:
  • What is the history of serigraphy?
  • How do ideas of industrialization relate to the print?
  • Why would someone want to repeat an image over and over again?
  • How could you co-opt this idea?

Artists considered: Andy Warhol’s Screen prints, Eduardo Paolozzi’s multi layer silkscreens, Sister Corita Kent’s work, Kara Walker’s prints

Assignment 3 prep:
  1. Sign up for a screen
  2. Before next class watch this video from the Tate Museum on silkscreen
  3. Look for examples of silkscreen printmaking and find one artist that interests you
  4. Find or create an image in separated layers that you will use for printing, your image should be drawn or printed on a transparency / or oil coated paper from a photocopier

Silkscreen Demo Day:
screen coating, preparing your image, exposure time / exposure unit basics, washing your image out of the screen, screen preparation before print, basics on registration, troubleshooting ink flow, flooding screens

Assignment 3: Silkscreen Printing
Applying the theme of Multiplicity print a variable edition of ten silkscreen prints This print should include at least (if not more!) two colors / layers, black and one other color, the other color can be any color you can find or mix at the print shop.

Discussion on Fix:
  • What is the glue that bonds us together?
  • How could we consider permanence and positioning?
  • How can you have movement and consistency together?

Artists Considered: Sara Greenberger Rafferty, Betty Woodman, Henri Matisse's cutouts, Robert Blackburn’s Subway Murals, Chakaia Booker

Assignment 4 Prep:
  1. Gather materials, make sure you have gampi and fabriano paper pack
  2. Take a look at the Our Processes Page under Chine collé at Wingate Press’ website
  3. Look through a collection of a museum for examples of Chine collé ie. MoMA’s collection
  4. Make some early sketches of ideas about shape, color and form for these prints

Chine collé Demo Day:
how to coat paper with melthyl-cellulose and dry, cutting shapes, wetting the printing paper and press settings, registration aids

Assignment 4: Chine Collé Printing
Applying the theme of fix create a variable edition or mono type chine collé prints numbering 4 in total. The print edition should have five colors, one being the white of the paper, and four additional colors, no other ink is needed in this process, the prints will be made with only paper and glue. Final Project
Student meetings will decide a theme of their final printmaking projects and create a one page proposal ahead of class. Bring this proposal including your projected timeline and materials needed. There will be individual meeting to discuss final project plans with instructor and AT and concepts, potential troubleshooting to go over final assignment, Also! bring your Sketchbook for review in this meeting.

Final Assignment:
For your Final Assignment you choose a combination of the techniques we have learned in class (at least two) and create a body of work of 5 prints, two of those prints could be from (and / or) build on completed assignments in class. This project should have a clear focus in terms of style, concept and execution. You should create a work plan for yourself to be able to complete the final assignment with timeline, and what materials you will need. You should be spending plenty of time outside of class to organize and complete this final assignment. You should also be considering a way to include in your artists statement a paragraph about this body of work.
